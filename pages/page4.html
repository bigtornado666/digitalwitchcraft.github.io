From ancient times to the present, there has been a belief rooted in the archetype of the mother, which is why the concept of Mother Earth exists. The Empress card represents either a mother figure or nature itself. Nature, being an embodiment of the mother archetype, nurtures and sustains various forms of life, often referred to as the Mother Earth. So, why is she called the Mother Earth, representing mothers? Firstly, in traditional Tarot cards, the Empress is depicted as voluptuous, implying that she carries the potential for new life within her. Even from a numerical perspective, "3" is the product of combining "1" (representing the masculine, the Magician) and "2" (representing the feminine, the High Priestess). "3" naturally symbolizes the child born from the union of male and female. In summary, the Empress can simultaneously represent motherhood and production, often indicating abundance, much like the mature fields of wheat and thriving waterfall forests. In Greek mythology, we associate the Empress card with Venus, the goddess of love and beauty, and Demeter, the goddess of agriculture.

In contemporary interpretations, the Empress is often associated with nurturing art, creative expression, aesthetics, and beauty. The widely shared platform for artistic creation by the public is an accurate reflection of the Empress card in the digital age of humanity.

Online art community platforms like Magma Drawing, Sketch, and Pinterest, all represent digital platforms where individuals with an interest in art gather to showcase their creative works, connect with fellow artists, and engage in artistic discussions. These communities provide a virtual space for artists to share their portfolios, receive feedback, and explore diverse forms of artistic expression. Through features such as forums, galleries, and interactive discussions, participants can connect with like-minded individuals, collaborate on projects, and stay updated on the latest trends and techniques in the art world. These online hubs foster a sense of community and support, breaking down geographical barriers and allowing artists from various backgrounds to come together in a shared space dedicated to the celebration and promotion of art.
