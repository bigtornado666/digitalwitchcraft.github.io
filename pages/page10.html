The Hermit card here takes on the role of a prophet. In traditional Rider-Waite Tarot, the robed figure of the Hermit embodies the energy of a seer, often cautioning people about the need for solitude for introspection. The Hermit illuminates the dim surroundings with the light in his hand, allowing him to see the subtle aspects of life. The Hermit represents a temporary departure from the external world to awaken the inner self and listen to one's own inner voice. This process requires maintaining silence, being still like water, undisturbed by external influences, emphasizing the crystallization of intelligence and absolute discipline. It is also a card representing inner success, maturity, hinting at the end of a cycle where you need to personally experience certain things, following the guidance of a higher self.
